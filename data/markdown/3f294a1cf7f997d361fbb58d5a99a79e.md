8. 
A conjoint reading of Sections 16 ail 20 CPC reveals that
where the dispute pertains to any right or interest in immovable
property, the special provision of Section 16 CPC prevails, and
the suit is to be instituted before the Court within whose territorial
jurisdiction the property is situated. Section 20 CPC, being
general in nature, cannot override the mandate of Section 16 CPC.
In the present case, the owner resides in District Peshawar, the
suit property is situated in Jalozai Housing Scheme at Tehsil
Pabbi, District Nowshera, and the office of the Provincial Housing
Authority, Khyber Pakhtunkhwa situated at Peshawar maintains
the official record pertaining to allotment and transfer of plots,
including the disputed plot situated at Jalozai Housing Scheme.
The alleged fraudulent act of defendant No.l, who is merely the
petitioner's brother, resident of District Bannu, and not owner of
the property, does not create any cause of action under Section
20(c) CPC nor confer jurisdiction upon the Court at Bannu. Since
the controversy essentially relates to rights in immovable
property, only the Court within whose teritorial limits the
property is situated has jurisdiction to adjudicate the matter.
g. 
Leamed counsel for the petitioner laid considerable
emphasis upon the alleged execution of the sale deed dated
13.08.2013, payment of sale consideration, and subsequent
arbitration proceedings, all allegedly took place at Bannu;
however, in view of the foregoing, these events do not advance
the petitioner's case for the simple reason that the claim
substantially relates to rights and interests in immovable property
situated at Tehsil Pabbi, District Nowshera.
