ABDUL FAYAZ, J.- Through the instant appeal, 
preferred under Section 410 of the Code of Criminal 
Procedure, 1898 read with Section 24 of the Khyber 
Pakhtunkhwa Control of Narcotic Substances Act, 
2019, the appellant, Sharafat Kazmi, has assailed the 
judgment dated 21.06.2025 passed by the learned 
Additional Sessions Judge-II/Special Judge, Mardan, 
in case FIR No.65 dated 22.08.2023, registered under 
Section 9(d) of the Khyber Pakhtunkhwa Control of 
Narcotic Substances Act, 2019 at Police Station 
Excise, Mardan. By the impugned judgment, the 
appellant was convicted under Section 9(d) of the Act 
ibid and sentenced to imprisonment for life with a fine 
of Rs.1,000,000/- (Rupees One Million) and, in default 
of payment thereof, to further undergo simple 
imprisonment for six months. Benefit of Section 382-B 
Cr.P.C. was also extended to him. 
2.

unfolded in the Murasila (Ex.PA/1), is that on

Upon completion of the investigation, the

Special Court. A formal charge was framed against

In order to substantiate the charge, the prosecution

5.  
Before embarking upon the merits of the 
controversy, it would be advantageous to briefly  
advert to the background of the present proceedings. 
The appellant was initially convicted and sentenced by 
the learned Additional Sessions Judge-IX, Mardan, 
vide judgment dated 22.07.2024. Feeling aggrieved, he 
preferred an appeal before this Court. The said appeal 
was disposed of vide judgment dated 30.01.2025, 
whereby the matter was remanded to the learned trial 
Court for the limited purpose of formally exhibiting 
certain documents which had inadvertently remained 
unmarked during trial and thereafter recording a fresh 
statement of the appellant under Section 342 Cr.P.C. 
with reference thereto. In compliance with the 
directions issued by this Court, the learned trial Court 
recalled the Investigating Officer, recorded his 
statement as RPW-05, formally exhibited the relevant 
documents, re-examined the appellant under Section 
342 Cr.P.C, and thereafter, upon fresh appraisal of the 
entire evidence, rendered the impugned judgment 
dated 21.06.2025, which is the subject matter of the 
present appeal.

6.  
Perusal of record reveals that on 22.08.2023 
the appellant, while driving Honda Motorcar bearing 
Registration No. LEJ-6121, was intercepted by the 
Excise Police during routine patrolling and checking at 
Marhati Road near Wali Interchange within the 
jurisdiction of Police Station Excise, Mardan. The 
appellant was found to be the sole occupant and driver 
of the vehicle. During interrogation, he disclosed the 
presence of narcotics concealed in the luggage 
compartment (diggi) of the vehicle. Acting upon such 
disclosure, the Excise officials recovered three plastic 
sacks containing sixty packets of charas, each 
weighing 1,200 grams, making the total recovery 72 
kilograms. Samples were drawn from each packet in 
accordance with the prescribed procedure, sealed 
separately with the official seal bearing the monogram 
"E&T", while the remaining bulk quantity was also 
sealed at the spot. The entire recovery proceedings 
were contemporaneously reduced into writing vide 
Recovery Memo (Ex.PW-2/1), leaving little room for 
any embellishment or fabrication.

any infirmity in the prosecution evidence regarding the

11. 
Similarly without substance is the contention 
that no private witness was associated with the 
recovery proceedings. The law is now well settled that 
evidence cannot be discarded merely because it 
emanates from police or Excise officials. Their 
testimony is to be evaluated on the same touchstone as 
that of any other witness. Where such evidence is 
found trustworthy, confidence-inspiring and free from 
any ulterior motive, conviction can safely be founded 
thereon even in the absence of independent witnesses. 
In the present case, the recovery was effected during 
routine checking on a public road, and there is nothing 
on record to suggest any prior acquaintance, enmity or 
motive on the part of the Excise officials to falsely 
implicate the appellant, who admittedly belongs to

Muzaffarabad, 
Azad 
Kashmir. 
It 
is 
wholly 
inconceivable that the officials would fabricate a case 
involving as much as 72 kilograms of charas against  
an absolute stranger by planting such an enormous 
quantity of narcotics. 
12. 
The defence further attempted to derive 
benefit from the alleged variation in the weight of the 
recovered contraband when produced before the Court. 
We find no substance in this contention. The alleged 
difference is insignificant and stands reasonably 
explained by the natural process of evaporation, drying 
and the passage of time. Such minor variation neither 
affects the identity of the recovered substance nor  
casts any doubt upon the prosecution case, particularly 
when the seals remained intact and the Forensic 
Science Laboratory confirmed the samples to be 
charas. Likewise, the objection regarding the shape or 
appearance of the packets after production before the 
Court is of no legal consequence, as slight changes in 
shape or packing owing to handling and storage do not 
detract from the otherwise overwhelming evidence 
establishing recovery and safe custody. 
13. 
It is an admitted position that the appellant 
was the sole occupant and driver of the vehicle from 
whose luggage compartment the narcotics were 
recovered. The vehicle remained in his exclusive

10
control and possession. In such circumstances, 
conscious possession stands fully established. Once 
exclusive possession is proved, the burden shifts upon 
the accused to furnish a plausible explanation 
regarding the presence of the contraband. The 
appellant, however, failed to offer any explanation 
whatsoever either during investigation or in his 
statement recorded under Section 342 Cr.P.C. He 
merely denied the prosecution allegations in a bald 
manner, did not opt to make a statement on oath under 
Section 340(2) Cr.P.C., nor did he produce any 
defence evidence. Such bare denial cannot outweigh 
the overwhelming prosecution evidence. 
14. 
It 
is 
equally 
settled 
that 
defects 
or 
irregularities, if any, in the investigation do not by 
themselves furnish a ground for acquittal unless it is 
shown that such defects have caused serious prejudice 
to the accused or have rendered the prosecution case 
doubtful. In the present case, even assuming some 
procedural lapse occurred during investigation, the 
same stands eclipsed by the direct, cogent and 
trustworthy ocular account, duly corroborated by 
documentary as well as scientific evidence. No 
instance of misreading, non-reading or suppression of 
material evidence has been pointed out by the learned 
defence counsel.

15. 
Having independently re-appraised the entire 
evidence, we are fully satisfied that the prosecution has 
succeeded in establishing beyond reasonable doubt 
that the appellant was found in conscious and 
exclusive possession of 72 kilograms of charas, 
thereby rendering himself liable under Section 9(d) of 
the Khyber Pakhtunkhwa Control of Narcotic 
Substances Act, 2019. The learned trial Court has 
appreciated the evidence in its true perspective and has 
recorded findings which are firmly anchored in the 
record. We have not been able to detect any illegality, 
perversity, misreading or non-reading of evidence 
warranting interference in exercise of appellate 
jurisdiction. 
16. 
As regards the sentence, we are of the 
considered opinion that the learned trial Court 
exercised its discretion judiciously. Considering the 
colossal quantity of narcotics recovered from the 
possession of the appellant and the grave menace 
posed by narcotic trafficking to society, the sentence of 
imprisonment for life with fine cannot by any stretch 
of imagination be termed harsh, excessive or 
disproportionate. Rather, the learned trial Court has 
already shown considerable leniency by not awarding 
the 
maximum 
sentence 
prescribed 
by 
law. 
Consequently, 
the

awarded

17. 
For the foregoing reasons, we hold that the 
prosecution has proved its case against the appellant 
beyond reasonable doubt through cogent, reliable and 
confidence-inspiring evidence, duly corroborated by 
unimpeachable documentary and scientific evidence. 
The impugned judgment dated 21.06.2025 passed by 
the learned Additional Sessions Judge-II/Special 
Judge, Mardan, is based upon a correct appraisal of the 
evidence and the applicable law and suffers from no 
illegality, infirmity or perversity. Consequently, 
finding no merit in this appeal, the same is dismissed, 
and the conviction and sentence awarded to the 
appellant are affirmed.
