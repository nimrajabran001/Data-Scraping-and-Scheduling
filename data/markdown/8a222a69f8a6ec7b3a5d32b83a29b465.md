14. 
 
A plain reading of the above provision leaves 
little room for doubt that where a statute repeals and re-enacts, 
with or without modification, the provisions of an earlier 
enactment, any reference in another enactment to the repealed 
law must ordinarily be construed as a reference to the re-
enacted provision, unless a contrary legislative intention is 
evident. Section 8 embodies the principle of legislative 
continuity. Its purpose is to prevent statutory references from 
becoming ineffective and redundant merely because the 
legislation governing a particular field has been repealed and 
replaced. The provision thus preserves continuity within the 
legal framework and safeguards remedies, forums, procedures 
and statutory mechanisms dependent upon cross-references to 
enactments that have subsequently undergone lapse, repeal 
and of course re-enactment / replacement. Consequently, 
where a statute confers a remedy or jurisdiction by reference 
to another enactment, and the latter is  replaced  by a successor 
statute  occupying  substantially  the  same  field,  the
